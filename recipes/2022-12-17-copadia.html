Ancient roman Copadia

The copadia is an ancient roman recipe featuring some classic ingredients like garum and defrutum. It is filling and flavorful, but takes a lot of time to make from scratch.

This variant of the classical Copadia was taken from Historical Italian Cooking: Beef Stew (Copadia)

Ingredients

  • Garum. Alternatively, use fish sauce or squid sacue
  • Defrutum. It's reduced wine juice, strained or unstrained
  • A cheap cut of meat, diced into large chunk
  • Roasted, peeled almonds
  • A red onion
  • A cople of dried dates
  • Black pepper
  • Parsley
  • Vinegar, preferably red wine vinegar

Steps

  1. Roast the almonds if you haven't done so. Then set them aside, and crush them.
  2. On the very same mortar add the black pepper and the dates. Crush them. The date can be crushed only lightly.
  3. Slice the onion as you like and set it to fry on a pot.
  4. Add the meat a few second after.
  5. Add the ground items.
  6. Add the garum, vinegar, and defrutum.
  7. While it simmers, cut the parsley and set it aside.
  8. There is no set cooking time. Let it reduce, the more it cooks without burning the better it'll be.
